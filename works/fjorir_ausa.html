3D Art

Cult of Fjorir Ausa.

Year

2022

Project Type

3D Prop Art

Duration

3 weeks

Overview

Independent project made as part of a 3D pop creation challenge, I had to come up with an idea and concept for the props and sculpt them from scratch, add materials, light and then render them in an real time time engine, I used Zbrush, substance painter and Blender eevee engine respectively

Lore

The Cult Worships the four eyed Dragon named Fjórir Ausa, who was sealed away long ago but is prophecied to return and bring with it the end of the world. Cult's worshippers posses artifacts connected to the dragon, a mask and a magical staff, both bearing the purple gems that come from the cave where Fjórir Ausa is said to be trapped in. The cult has commited many atrosities in the name of the forgotten dragon and is working towards reviving the it, bringing about the end of the world

Unreal Engine 5

Blender

Substance

Introduction

The aim of the project was to try an experiment to work with concept sculpts, departing from traditional 2D concept workflows, supported with a detailed narrative to guide the visuals centered around the lore of the Cult of Fjórir Ausa. The goal was to execute a full pipeline from narrative to final execution, and see what different approaches affect it in which ways.

The Staff

Concept

Wielded by the cult leader, the staff of Fjórir is said to contain pieces of the soul of Fjórir Ausa, hence it is said to have a mind of its own and chooses its owner itself. When grabbed by the handle, the thorns pierce the hand of the wielder, forming a blood bond between the two, and in most cases takes over the mind of the wielder controlling them for as long.

Sculpt

Playing with different forms and silhouettes lead to the concept sculpt, slowly being refined and reflecting the lore of the cult.

The Mask

Given to every member of the cult at the time of initiation, this four eyed mask made of bone is said to open the eyes of the soul of each person wearing the mask at the time of awakening of Fjórir Ausa the four eyed dragon that has been trapped away for centuries.

Sculpt

References of animals were used to come up with the form of the mask, reflecting the inhumane nature of the cult members. Eventually refined and engraved with runic patterns.

Post Mortem

Strengths

  • Many details came into being during the process of creation itself, creating a feedback loop, where the narrative affected the design, and the design ending up changing the narrative.
  • The process started with recognizable silhouettes that allowed the overall design to be visually distinct. Making sure the silhouette worked from all the major views and not just from the front and sides that 2D concept art contributes.
  • The process allowed me to think easily in 3 dimensions in relation to each other, allowing me to work with the props with a relational depth that had been lacking when working with 2D concept art.
  • Working directly in 3D also allowed me to experiment with texture and tactility simultaneously with the form, ensuring both complimented each other.

Weaknesses

  • Fixing mistakes and rapid experimentation directly in 3D was time-consuming, and therefore discouraging it further, where at times when a design decision was implemented and not too out of place, I was rarely trying out different approaches.
  • The narrative was self-contained, where the story and details only revolved around the current props, and not being connected to a larger world and other aspects of it.

Insights

  • A hybrid approach would result in the best result, bringing in the best of both worlds, it occurred to me that a member working on a 2D concept simultaneously, while considered inefficient, could result in different ideas being merged together, taking advantage of both 2D and 3D, while limiting the biases leaking in. Or a single person going back and forth.
  • Modular sculpting could also allow for rapid prototyping, ensuring we take 3D advantages, while saving time and allowing for multiple iterations and quick changes.
  • Secondary props, even if not designed or sculpted, could enhance the depth of the story and allow the props that were created to be even richer in detail.
