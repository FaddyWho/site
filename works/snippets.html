Year

2024

Project Type

App Design

Duration

5 weeks

Overview

Snippets is a supplementary language learning app, with a focus on vocabulary. It uses augmented reality and artificial intelligence to help users identify objects in their proximity and learn the the words for it. Users can also contribute to the vocabulary database by tagging objects in the language they know and review tags contributed by other users to help expand the vocabulary database and improve the AI recognition.

Problem

Learning vocabulary is difficult and users often lose interest in standardised courses which aren't catered to their lifestyle and needs.

Solution

Design an app that uses modern technologies like AI and AR to create custom learning experiences based on each user's lifestyle and proximity to objects they use everyday.

Figma

Introduction

Language preservation

The idea for a language learning app started with the realization that regional languages and dialects are slowly fading away. While modernization gives us many benefits, it also takes away the intricacies of regional culture are overtaken by a unified metropolitan society. This is clearly evident in Pakistani society where learning English takes precedence over mastering your own mother tongue, many people cannot speak Urdu or Sindhi without interjecting borrowed words from English where there is no need to.

To preserve and promote regional languages and dialects, and allow people to easily learn them I decided to create Snippets. Which would take advantage of AI and also help solve the problems it faces.

Shortcomings of Artificial Intelligence

Large language models, currently face an array of problems which causes it to output false information, or have a lack of contextual understanding. This is caused by a variety of factors including but not limited to;

  • Bias in training data
  • Limited real-time knowledge
  • Lack of niche or specialized data

Snippets was built to solve these two problems simultaneously. Where documenting language would also help improve AI in a way that they compliment each other.

Research and Insights

Understanding the Users

As part of the user research process, I conducted surveys to identify common challenges in language learning, gaining insights into the difficulties learners face. Additionally, I analyzed existing language learning apps to pinpoint gaps and areas for improvement. To further refine the research, I interviewed language learners to understand their preferences, needs, and expectations, ensuring that the findings were user-centered and actionable.

Key Findings

  • Users found visual and contextual learning more effective than rote memorization.
  • There was a strong interest in gamification and community-driven learning.
  • Many users struggled with retaining vocabulary not used in daily life.

Competitive analysis

A competitive analysis of language learning apps revealed same issues that were mentioned in the survey and the interviews. The apps follow a standardized course, teaching vocabulary that is often irrelevant to the users' current context. Similarly, they often keep focus of the users on the screen, where lessons get blurred together. While they use graphics and gamification elements, they still lack any social features to connect users to fellow learners.

This prompted me to try a different approach to helping users learn vocabulary, where these issues are addressed and solved using two learning strategies, namely proximity learning, and spatial mnemonics.

Design Strategies

Proximity Learning

Spatial Nuemonics

Proximity Learning

Contrary to a standard course, the app would focus on teaching words for objects that users find around themselves and interact with on a daily bases, similar to a child who learns words as they become important in their lives and will be used recurringly.

Spatial Mnemonics

The app would display the name of the object in augmented reality on top of the object seen through the phone, this engages user's spatial memory and the names can be easily recalled while seeing the object in real life similar to Method of loci. This idea is supported by the situated cognition theory (1989).

Based on these findings, Snippets was built with the following guiding principles:

  • Context-based learning: Users learn words that are immediately useful in their lives.
  • Spatial Mnemonics: User retain words better associated in physical space rather than a flat screen, augmented reality can be used to achieve this.
  • Gamification elements: Users earn points for learning, tagging, and reviewing objects.
  • Community-driven approach: The database grows through user contributions, and allows them to connect with fellow learners.

Design Process

Systems Development

System Flow


The app was designed to function in a feedback loop, where users earn points by tagging objects and reviewing contributions. Which in turn teaches learners said language processed through the large language model. This enhances the AI's accuracy.

  • Object Recognition via AI: Users scan objects with their camera, and the AI suggests the corresponding word in language they are trying to learn.
  • Tagging & Reviewing: Users can contribute new words or validate existing ones to maintain accuracy.
  • Points System: Users earn and spend points by using either of the previous features, maintaining a balance of contribution and learning.

AR Navigation

Objects highlighted in blue are to be selected while object in yellow are next in line based on proximity to the hovered one.

Once an object is selected, the highlight inside the object to select smaller parts of it which is controlled by different arrows color will turn to green, and users can go.


Developing the Augmented Reality navigation and interaction was the most interesting aspect of designing for the app. The view through the app can be unpredictable, with multiple objects simultaneously visible in the surrounding of the object, as well as objects being defined in a way that is similar to a russian doll. Every object is made up of smaller objects, which is why it was necessary to create a way focus an object, and then go through its inner selection. This all depends on the view scale of the camera as well.

Learning and Takeaways

Critical Number of Users.

Vocabulary expansion may initially favor regions with high user numbers, taking over smaller dialects and reflecting the problems diversity of regional languages faces in real life.

Region-based language selection would allow information to be easily categorized, and languages with smaller user bases should be given extra support so that more learners can join and reach a critical number of users.

Relying on User Generated Data.

Quality and diversity of vocabulary relies on active and accurate user participation, allowing the possibility of misuse and false information being spread among users.

Moderation would be required so only correct information is shared, and a reputation-based score could be implemented that would discourage misuse and allow users to earn a good standing in the community.

AR accessibility and Alternatives

Feasibility of of AR navigation is untested and would require developmental input. It can also be challenging for new and non-tech-savvy users, making it harder for the app to reach critical user numbers which it heavily relies on. This would also exclude users with older and less powerful devices, exacerbating the problem further.

Alternative ways to interact with the app would allow a variety of users to engage with it easily, such as picture-based submissions, solving both the non-tech-savvy user and older device problem simultaneously.
