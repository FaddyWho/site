Conceptual Architecture

Sleeve Factory.

Year

2020

Project Type

Architecture

Duration

5 weeks

Overview

This was my fourth-year architectural world-view project where we were given creative freedom to choose our own project and create it from scratch. I decided to build a functional post-apocalyptic scenario and build a human cloning factory from the perspective of a resident architect of that city.

Lore

Facing an ecological disaster that made Earth uninhabitable, the wealthy built bunkers and self-sustaining cities, leading to the creation of the domed city. The city was divided into two districts: the surface, where citizens lived, and the underground, initially maintained by androids. Over time, as resources dwindled and androids failed, the city allowed survivors, called immigrants, to replace them. These immigrants were confined to the underground and tasked with keeping the city running, barred from ever living on the surface.

Introduction

Simulation theory and determinism challenge our perception of reality and prompt us to question the conditions we take for granted. They raise a central speculative question: What if the parameters of our world were different? How would this shift affect human society—and more specifically, architecture? As a reflection of its context, architecture is shaped by environmental, social, and cultural forces. By imagining alternate realities with different underlying conditions, we not only expand the boundaries of design speculation but also gain a deeper understanding of how our current world shapes architectural expression. This process reveals how deeply human systems and built environments are bound to both inherent tendencies and imposed structures—offering a critical lens through which to rethink the possible.

The dome has become a symbol of protection, which is supported by the central government tower, telling its citizens if it collapses, so does the dome.

The division between the districts is visually obvious, the surface resembling a modern utopia while the underground a cyberpunk nightmare. However the lack of control in the underground also gives it its charm, personality and freedom of experssion.

Building the Sleeve Factory

The project is concieved as a government-commissioned architect designing a sleeve factory. The facility spans from the surface to the underground. On the surface, luxury pods collect citizens’ DNA and later serve as venues for transferring consciousness into new bodies. Underground, a high-security factory, supervised by androids and sta ed by immigrants, manufactures the sleeves.

Form

Form process

Due to the limited footprint of the of the dome, the city was forced to grow vertically, adding a third dimension to the already complex city. eventually acquiring third level high up in the skyline, it was easier to travel between buildings, rather than going all the way down. soon the city had small streets connecting the towers in its skyline.
