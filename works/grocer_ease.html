UX/UI

Grocer Ease.

Team

Solo

Project Type

UX/UI

Duration

3 weeks

Overview

Grocer-Ease is an app that helps users plan for grocery shopping in advance, navigate the store and simplify the checkout experience. The typical user is between 18-40 years old, and is someone who is moved to a new city. The goal is to simplify and streamline the shopping experience

Problem

Grocery shopping is a time consuming experience, which without proper planning can cost more time and money, especially if the stores are large which can make it hard to navigate.

Solution

Design an app that help users plan their grocery shopping and navigate through large stores to help streamline the process, saving both time and money.

Figma

Reseach and Insights

User Surveys and Interviews

User reseach was conducted to find shopping habits, preferences, pain points and the efficacy of the project itself. Findings revealed critial information that challenged my pre-concieved notions of users preferences, which in turn informed the design aproach and required features.

Key Findings
  • 40% of users prefer shopping in person while 50% prefer a hybrid shopping approach (online + in-store).
  • Over 50% don't plan their shopping through lists—they browse in store.
  • Checkout is a major pain point, often disliked or found tedious.
  • Users who move to new cities have trouble navigating large store.

Insights and Pain Points

The research revealed the application to be viable. However, as opposed to my previous aproach, features priority needs to be shifted towards user needs and habits. Following are the major pain points that users face.

Long, frustrating checkout lines.
Unpredictable item availability.
Large store are confusing to navigate.

User Personas

Using the interviews and the survey data, I created 2 user personas that represented the average users's goals, concerns and their shopping behaivior. User persona A Ayesha Kaleem, represents young students, who are native to the city they reside in and enjoy grocery shopping. User persona B Ahmed Hassan represents older busier users, who are new to the city they reside in and often see grocery shopping as a chore and prefer efficiency in it.

Features/Reccomendations

Based on the pain points and the user personas created through interviews and survey data, I reccomend three main features that need to be implemented in the app in order of priority.

  • Scan-as-you-go and digital checkout options.
  • Smart grocery list with availability info.
  • In-app store maps with guided item paths.

My initial aproach focused more on preplanned grocery list and scanning products based on the said list. However, based on user persona A, people who dont plan groceries and shop as they go, I changed that aproach to include scanning products as needed.

Improvements and Learnings

Optimizing map readability.

Users revealed the navigation map was hard to read and aesthetically inpleasing. Further steps need to be taken to improve the visuals and icons to make it easier to read and pleasing to navigate.

Streamlined experience for users who prefer to browse and scan, vs user who need the maps.

Currently the apps forces both types of users to go through the navigation screen, even if they just need to scan and don't need the map feature, this causes a clutered screen where the map takes up majority of the screen. A separate screen or ability to minimize the map is needed so that users can focus on their preference of using the app.

Potential for additional users who are visually impaired.

Improving upon the navigation and scanning features the app has huge potential to be useful to visually impaired users. Following are some features that can be implemented after additional user research;

  • Adding voice commands to add products to grocery list.
  • Adding audio navigation.
  • Scans revealing audible product information.
